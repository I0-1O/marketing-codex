# Buyer Voice Intelligence

## Definition

The practice of mining sales call recordings and conversation intelligence platforms to extract the exact language buyers use when evaluating, objecting, and comparing vendors — then embedding that language directly into sales and marketing materials.

## Why It Matters

PMMs write battlecards and messaging in marketing language. Buyers think and speak in buyer language. The gap between those two produces materials that reps find credible in theory but awkward to use in practice. Buyer voice intelligence closes that gap at the source: instead of inferring what buyers care about, you transcribe it.

The effect, per Doherty: battlecard language that makes "your sales team sound like they're reading the prospect's mind."

## How It Works

### Four Intelligence Categories (Doherty)

1. **Objection triggers** — what competitive claims or rep statements consistently provoke pushback
2. **Feature perception gaps** — where buyers perceive a feature as weaker or stronger than it actually is (perception is what matters in a deal)
3. **Pricing intelligence** — how buyers frame and contextualize price; what anchors they use
4. **Authentic buyer vocabulary** — the exact words and phrases buyers use to describe the problem and the category

### Sources

- Conversation intelligence platforms (Gong, Chorus/ZoomInfo, etc.) — the primary source; recorded sales calls
- Win/loss interviews — slower but richer; structured conversation with recent buyers and churned customers
- G2 / review platforms — buyer-written reviews use buyer language; useful for objection and vocabulary mining at scale

### Application

Extracted buyer language flows directly into:
- Battlecard talk tracks and objection-rebuttal pairs
- Messaging frameworks (customer voice section)
- Website copy and campaign headlines
- Sales email sequences

## Connection to Messaging Principles

Buyer voice intelligence is the research method that makes [[problem-first-messaging]] operational. Sitting in the problem is a discipline — buyer voice intelligence is the tool that shows you what the problem actually sounds like from the buyer's side.

It also connects to [[marketing-voice-and-pov]]: brand voice and buyer voice are different things. PMM work requires holding both simultaneously — your voice shaped by your position, your language shaped by your buyer.

## Limitations

- Conversation intelligence requires the infrastructure to be in place (sales team using the platform consistently)
- Review platform language skews toward customers who feel strongly (positive or negative) — not representative of the median buyer
- Buyer vocabulary shifts as the category matures; intelligence needs ongoing refresh
