# AI Voice Tells in Marketing Copy

## Definition

Structural and linguistic patterns that signal AI-generated (or unedited AI-drafted) marketing copy. These tells appear because LLMs optimize for completeness and inoffensiveness — not voice, conviction, or story. They are a snapshot of patterns observed 2024–2026; specific signals will evolve as LLMs improve.

## Why It Matters

Audiences have developed a nonconscious sense for AI-generated content. Research from the Nuremberg Institute for Market Decisions (NIM, 2024) found a measurable "trust penalty": identical content is rated as less credible, less appealing, and less natural when labeled as AI-generated. The signal doesn't need to be labeled — readers feel it. When they feel it, trust drops.

## The Six Tells

**1. Em dashes as connective tissue**
Em dashes connect clauses without committing to sentence structure. In marketing copy, almost always means the sentence should have been two sentences — or the writer let the model meander.

**2. Comma chains**
Long sentences with three or four clauses joined by commas. Technically grammatical. Practically unreadable. If a sentence has more than one comma, it probably needs to be split.

**3. The buzzword layer**
Words that sound impressive but communicate nothing: *seamless, robust, leverage, harness, empower, unlock, streamline, transformative, holistic, cutting-edge, next-generation, best-in-class*. If a word works equally well in your copy and your competitor's copy, it isn't doing anything.

**4. Circular structure**
Paragraph opens with a claim. Middle restates it with different words. End restates it again as a conclusion. Three sentences, one idea, zero progression. The paragraph feels complete but never went anywhere.

**5. Hedging openers**
"It's worth noting that..." / "It's important to remember..." / "Ultimately..." — throat-clearing that adds words without adding meaning. Cut every time.

**6. Symmetrical structure (over-parallel construction)**
Three pillars, each with exactly three bullets, each bullet the same length. Real ideas aren't that tidy. When content looks stamped from a mold, readers feel it. Note: parallel structure itself isn't a tell — over-application is the signal, especially in aggregate with other factors.

## Before / After Example

**AI default (circular structure):**
> Digital transformation requires organizations to rethink how they approach process automation. By reimagining their automation strategy, companies can unlock new efficiencies and drive meaningful change. This transformation of processes through automation enables organizations to achieve their digital objectives more effectively.

**Rewritten (position + progression):**
> Most automation projects start with the wrong question. Teams ask "what can we automate?" instead of "what's actually broken?" Start with the process that's costing you the most time, money, or frustration. Automate that one first.

## How to Use This in Practice

Apply as an editing checklist against any AI draft:
- Flag every em dash → rewrite as two sentences
- Flag every sentence with 2+ commas → split it
- Flag every buzzword → replace with specific, concrete language or cut
- Flag every circular paragraph → add a position or progression, or cut
- Flag every hedging opener → delete it, start with the actual claim
- Flag over-parallel structures → vary length and rhythm deliberately

Can also be loaded as prompt guardrails before or during AI drafting.
