# Marketing Voice and Point of View

## Definition

Brand voice in marketing copy is the product of deliberate exclusion — choosing what *not* to say as much as what to say. Good copy takes a position. It says "this matters and here's why" rather than "here are some things to consider." Voice is distinct from style; it's the accumulated effect of every specific choice about language, structure, and stance.

## Why It Matters

In a market where most companies sound identical, sounding different is a competitive advantage. Default AI copy can't create voice because its design is inclusion — it generates everything, hedges everything, offends no one. Voice requires commitment to a perspective. Companies with clear, specific voices earn trust and attention that generic copy cannot.

The NIM (2024) trust penalty research supports this: audiences react negatively to content that feels assembled rather than written — at both conscious and nonconscious levels.

## How It Works

**Voice comes from exclusion.** Every word you don't use is a choice. Every comparison you refuse to make is a choice. Every buzzword you ban is a choice. The sum of those choices is your voice.

**A position is not an opinion about the market — it's a stance embedded in every sentence.** Mailchimp's "we are plainspoken" isn't a tagline. It's a constraint that governs every word they publish. 37signals' combative tone isn't a personality quirk — it's a product of DHH's principle that his personal credibility is on the line for every word the company publishes.

**The seven editing rules** (from [[writing-for-humans-killing-ai-voice]]):
1. **Short sentences** — one comma max; read it out loud
2. **Second person** — "you" forces specificity; "organizations" enables vagueness
3. **Problem before solution** — lead with pain; the product earns attention after the problem earns recognition
4. **Bullets: 3 ideal, 5 ceiling** — a list of 7 is a paragraph that gave up
5. **Positive framing** — say what you do, not what competitors don't
6. **Read it out loud** — if you wouldn't say it in a meeting, don't write it
7. **One idea per paragraph** — two points = two paragraphs

## Examples

**[[mailchimp]]:** "We are plainspoken. We strip away hyperbolic language, upsells, and over-promises. We value clarity above all." — codified in an open-source public style guide. The voice works because it's specific and constrained.

**[[37signals]]:** DHH and Jason Fried write under their own names. Marketing reads like opinion pieces. Their Google Ads campaign calling out competitors earned CNBC coverage — not because it was polished, but because it had a point of view.

## AI as Drafting Tool

AI accelerates the first draft. It does not replace the last mile. The value is getting ideas on paper faster — from 30 minutes of blank-page staring to 10 minutes of having something to tear apart. Voice lives in the rewrite. The best AI-assisted copy is invisible: nobody can tell a machine helped.

The practical workflow:
1. Use AI to scaffold the draft
2. Apply the seven editing rules and the [[ai-voice-tells-in-marketing-copy]] checklist
3. Enforce a banned word list in every prompt and every edit
