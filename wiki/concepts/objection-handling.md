# Objection Handling

## Definition

The practice of anticipating, categorizing, and preparing responses to buyer resistance — whether about price, fit, timing, competitor preference, or internal politics. In a PMM context, objection handling is a messaging artifact: structured rebuttals backed by proof points, built into enablement materials.

## Why It Matters

Buyers raise objections at every stage of the funnel. PMMs who treat objection handling as an afterthought produce messaging that works in the abstract but collapses under real deal pressure. The best competitive positioning is pre-emptive objection handling — framing your story so the objection never forms.

## How It Works

Objection handling frameworks typically follow this structure:

1. **Acknowledge** — validate the concern without agreeing with it
2. **Reframe** — shift the evaluative criteria to ground where you win
3. **Evidence** — specific proof (customer quote, benchmark, case study)
4. **Bridge** — return to your core message

### Common Objection Categories

- **Price** — "You're more expensive." (Reframe to TCO or outcome value)
- **Competitor preference** — "We're already using X." (Switching cost vs. switching value)
- **Timing** — "We'll revisit next quarter." (Cost of inaction, urgency drivers)
- **Feature gap** — "X does Y and you don't." (Roadmap, workaround, or reframe relevance)
- **Risk** — "You're smaller / newer / unproven." (Reference accounts, stability signals)

## Sourcing Objections

The best objection content comes from buyer language, not PMM inference. [[buyer-voice-intelligence]] — mining sales call recordings via conversation intelligence platforms — surfaces the actual objections buyers raise and the exact vocabulary they use. Objection-rebuttal pairs built from this source are more credible and easier for reps to use than those written from product knowledge alone.

## In Battle Cards

Battle cards are the primary delivery vehicle for objection handling in sales contexts. Each card should include objection-response pairs specific to the named competitor — not generic rebuttals that could apply to anyone.

See [[battle-card]] for the full artifact spec.

## Connection to Messaging

Objection handling is downstream of positioning. If your [[master-messaging-document]] is built correctly, many objections resolve at the positioning level before the rep has to respond. Treat persistent objections as a signal that positioning has a gap.
