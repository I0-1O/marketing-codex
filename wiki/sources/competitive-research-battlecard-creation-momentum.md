A second battlecard prompt from Momentum's AI prompt library. More structured and comprehensive than [[competitor-research-battlecard-momentum]]. Uses the GRACE framework (copyright Coach K / Coach K Prompts).

## Full Output Structure

1. **SWOT analysis** — both companies
2. **Five winning strategies** — your unique selling points vs. this competitor
3. **Three topics to avoid** — things not to say when competing against them
4. **Five objections with counterarguments** — evidence-backed rebuttals
5. **Feature comparison chart** — 6+ features, side-by-side
6. **Four landmines** — competitor weaknesses to expose in deals
7. **Five follow-up strategies** — with example email copy
8. **Head-to-head comparison table** — 6 aspects

## Notable Elements

**"Topics to avoid"** is an underused section in standard battle cards. Forces the question: what framings or claims backfire against this specific competitor? Useful for training reps away from reflexive positioning that doesn't hold up.

**Follow-up sequences with email copy** extends the artifact beyond the conversation into post-meeting outreach — blurring the line between battlecard and sales playbook.

**GRACE framework** — proprietary to Coach K Prompts. Not enough public detail to document the framework itself; flagged for future research if the framework becomes relevant.

## PMM Notes

The "topics to avoid" section is the most distinct contribution of this prompt vs. standard battlecard formats. Most PMM battlecard templates focus on what to say — this adds explicit guidance on what not to say, which is often more valuable for trained reps who already know the pitch.

The email follow-up component makes this more of a competitive sales playbook than a pure battlecard.
