# Writing for Humans: Killing the AI Voice in Product Marketing

> Full text: [[writing-for-humans-killing-ai-voice]] (articles/)

## Synopsis

A practical guide arguing that the problem with AI-generated marketing copy isn't AI itself — it's accepting the default output. LLMs optimize for completeness and inoffensiveness, not voice or conviction. The article catalogs six specific structural tells of AI-generated copy, provides before/after rewrites, lays out seven concrete editing rules, and argues that brand voice comes from *exclusion* — from deciding what not to say — which default AI cannot do.

The central framing: AI is a drafting tool, not a finishing tool. The last mile is where voice lives.

## Key Arguments

- The NIM "trust penalty" study (2024): consumers rate identical content as less credible when told it's AI-generated — at both conscious and nonconscious levels
- Default AI copy optimizes for completeness; voice requires taking a position
- "Voice comes from exclusion. From deciding what doesn't belong." — the core principle
- [[mailchimp]] and [[37signals]] cited as examples of companies with deliberate, specific brand voice
