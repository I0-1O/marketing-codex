# Stop turning everything into an antithesis

**Source:** Marketing That Matters, issue #035 — [[jeff-angus]]

## Synopsis

Angus argues that "It's not X, it's Y" has become the defining AI marketing tell — and that its ubiquity signals not creative writing but statistical pattern completion. LLMs are trained on marketing copy saturated with antithesis constructions, so they reproduce them as the high-probability "authoritative" choice. The result: a self-reinforcing loop where overuse trains future models to use it more.

The rhetorical mechanism: negation (defining by what something isn't) combined with antithesis (dramatic contrast). It feels structured and persuasive but conceals lack of conviction. The actual problem isn't the structure itself — it's that it substitutes reframing for genuine category clarity.

The prescription: name your category, own it, improve it, acknowledge it exists. "Negation is easy. Clarity is harder."

## Key Points

- "It's not a gym, it's a movement" / "It's not a phone, it's a paradigm shift" — both duck the question of what the product actually does and for whom
- AI uses this because negation-plus-contrast is statistically safe: it feels like a position without requiring one
- The pattern masks weak positioning, not bad writing — the writing technique is fine; the positioning work is skipped
- Overuse has made this construction an immediate AI signal to readers, eroding trust before the message lands
