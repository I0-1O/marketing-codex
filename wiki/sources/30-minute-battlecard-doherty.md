# The 30-Minute Battlecard

Medium article by Thomas E Doherty arguing that AI has compressed battlecard creation from an 8-hour manual process to a 30-minute workflow. Published February 12, 2026. Doherty also authored "The AI-Accelerated GTM Playbook."

## Core Thesis

Not just speed — better outputs. AI pulls from more sources (G2 reviews, press releases, LinkedIn) faster than manual research, and conversation intelligence platforms surface authentic buyer language that PMMs can't get any other way.

## Three-Phase Methodology

**Phase 1: Competitive intelligence extraction (~10 min)**
- AI research tools synthesize from G2 reviews, press releases, LinkedIn
- Key: structured prompts, not vague queries
- Critical caveat: validate all AI-generated claims before publishing — hallucinations are common on pricing, feature specifics, customer names

**Phase 2: Buyer voice intelligence (~10 min)**
- Mine conversation intelligence platforms (sales call recordings)
- Four intelligence categories: objection triggers, feature perception gaps, pricing intelligence, authentic buyer vocabulary
- Goal: battlecard language that sounds like the rep is reading the prospect's mind

**Phase 3: Battlecard assembly (~5–10 min)**
- Feed Phase 1 + Phase 2 into AI writing tools
- Five output sections: positioning summary, differentiator matrix, talk track, objection-rebuttal pairs, comparison table
- Honest about competitive weaknesses — credibility over false superiority

## Five Battlecard Sections (Doherty's Structure)

1. Positioning summary
2. Differentiator matrix
3. Talk track
4. Objection-rebuttal pairs
5. Comparison table

## Economic Frame

For mid-market B2B SaaS, five competitors tracked quarterly:
- Traditional: 150–300 hours/year
- AI-accelerated: ~7.5 hours/year
- Delta redirects to market analysis, positioning, enablement programs

## Continuous Intelligence

Doherty frames the AI-accelerated workflow as enabling "continuous intelligence" — battlecards updated when competitive changes happen, not on a quarterly schedule. Frequency becomes a function of trigger (competitor news, lost deal pattern) rather than calendar.

## Key Rules

1. Validate AI-sourced claims before publishing
2. Use exact buyer language, not marketing jargon
3. Acknowledge competitor strengths honestly — false superiority destroys rep trust
4. Update on competitive trigger events, not just quarterly
