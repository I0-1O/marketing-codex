# Brian Rieb

## Overview

Principal Product Marketing Manager with deep experience in enterprise B2B software — specifically workflow automation and business process platforms. Author of this vault. Voice: direct, concise, no marketing fluff. Substance over polish.

## Key Facts

- Principal PMM, enterprise B2B software (workflow automation, business process platforms)
- Strong point of view on AI-assisted writing: AI is a drafting tool, not a finishing tool
- Known for enforcing strict copy rules: short sentences, second person, problem before solution, banned buzzword lists
- Self-described lover of parallel structure and the rule of three (acknowledged as a potential AI tell when overused)

## Writing Philosophy

From [[writing-for-humans-killing-ai-voice]]:
- Copy must have a position, not just information
- Voice comes from exclusion — deciding what *not* to say
- The last mile (rewriting the AI draft) is where voice lives
- Banned words include: em dashes, seamless, robust, harness, empower (and ~20 others)

## Positioning

Sits at the intersection of technical marketing and clear, human writing. Skeptical of default AI output. Advocates for using AI to accelerate drafting while preserving the human editorial layer.

## PMM Frameworks

- The [[benefit-ladder]]: Feature → Direct Effect → Business Outcome
- "So what?" test applied exactly once — two levels up is a platitude
- A decade reviewing enterprise software copy; the direct-effect-as-benefit pattern is the most common failure
- Has PM background — familiar with the proximity-to-product bias that causes the pattern
