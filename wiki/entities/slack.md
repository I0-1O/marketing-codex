# Slack

## Overview

Enterprise messaging and collaboration platform. Acquired by Salesforce in 2021. Used in this vault as a positioning case study illustrating the [[benefit-ladder]] — specifically, the evolution from a direct-effect positioning ("email killer") to a business-outcome positioning (faster decisions, searchable knowledge).

## Key Facts

- Early positioning: "replaces email," "reduce internal email 75%" — a direct effect
- Why companies actually adopted it: faster decisions (conversations not buried in inboxes), searchable knowledge, faster onboarding for new hires
- Messaging evolution: shifted toward "your productivity platform," stories about faster decision-making and team alignment — one tier up
- The pattern: the direct effect made good advertising; the business outcome drove adoption and conversion

## Positioning Case Study

Slack's early "email killer" positioning is a clean example of effective advertising built on a direct effect. It created attention and category awareness. But the conversion story — why companies actually committed and expanded — was the business outcome: organizational speed and knowledge accessibility. The two served different purposes at different stages of the funnel.
