# Clayton Christensen

## Overview

Harvard Business School professor and innovation theorist. Best known for disruptive innovation theory (*The Innovator's Dilemma*) and Jobs to Be Done (JTBD) theory. JTBD is widely applied in product marketing for understanding buyer motivation at the outcome level rather than the feature/attribute level.

## Key Facts

- Jobs to Be Done: customers don't buy products, they *hire* them to make progress in a specific circumstance
- The "progress" customers seek = the business outcome tier in the [[benefit-ladder]]
- Milkshake example: people hired milkshakes to make commutes tolerable and arrive not hungry — not for flavor or fullness
- Key works: *The Innovator's Dilemma* (1997), *Competing Against Luck* (2016, with Hall, Dillon, Duncan), HBR article "Know Your Customers' Jobs to Be Done" (September 2016)
- *Competing Against Luck* argues that focusing on demographics and product attributes leads to innovation failure; focusing on the job leads to products people want

## Positioning (in the PMM space)

JTBD is the most widely adopted framework for understanding *why* buyers buy — the motivational layer beneath stated preferences and product comparisons. Applied in PMM to inform messaging, positioning, and ICP work.
