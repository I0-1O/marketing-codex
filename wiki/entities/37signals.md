# 37signals

## Overview

Software company behind Basecamp (project management) and HEY (email). Known for founder-led marketing and a deliberately combative, opinionated brand voice. DHH (David Heinemeier Hansson) and Jason Fried write under their own names, making their marketing read like opinion pieces rather than product pages.

## Key Facts

- Products: Basecamp, HEY
- Founders: Jason Fried, David Heinemeier Hansson (DHH)
- DHH principle: "I substitute my name for whatever the company says, and I feel like it's my credibility on the line." — accountability drives every word
- Ran a Google Ads campaign calling out competitors for bidding on the Basecamp brand name; earned CNBC coverage not because it was polished, but because it had a point of view
- Marketing tone: direct, occasionally combative, always specific

## Positioning

Positioned as the anti-enterprise SaaS. Against complexity, bloat, and corporate-speak. Their marketing is a product of their product philosophy — simple, opinionated, human.

## Competitive Context

Competes with Asana, Monday.com, Notion, and others in project management. Differentiated by philosophical stance as much as product features.
