# Matthew Dixon & Brent Adamson

## Overview

Authors of *The Challenger Sale: Taking Control of the Customer Conversation* (2011, Portfolio/Penguin). Research-based framework identifying that top-performing B2B sales reps (Challengers) lead with commercial insights that reframe how customers think about their business, then connect those insights back to their product's unique strengths.

## Key Facts

- *The Challenger Sale* identified five rep profiles; Challengers significantly outperformed others, especially in complex sales
- "Teaching for differentiation": Challengers lead with outcome-level insights, not product capabilities
- Structure mirrors the [[benefit-ladder]] in reverse: start with the business outcome (the reframe/insight), connect back to the capability
- PMM implication: enablement materials written at the direct effect tier handicap Challenger reps — they need outcome-level messaging to teach with
- Followed by *The Challenger Customer* (2015), which extends the framework to multi-stakeholder deals

## Positioning (in the PMM space)

The Challenger Sale is the most-cited sales methodology in enterprise B2B. Its PMM implication is direct: the messaging and enablement you give reps determines whether they can sell at the outcome level or are stuck pitching features.
