# Barbara Minto

## Overview

Former McKinsey consultant and author of *The Pyramid Principle* (1987, updated editions through 2020). Developed the standard framework for structured business communication at major consulting firms: lead with the answer, support with key arguments, back with evidence. Top-down, conclusion-first.

## Key Facts

- *The Pyramid Principle: Logic in Writing and Thinking* (Pearson, 2020)
- Developed at McKinsey in the 1960s; remains the communication standard at major consulting firms
- Framework: BLUF (bottom line up front), then MECE-structured supporting arguments, then evidence
- Excellent for: written reports, executive memos, documents where the reader controls pace and may stop early
- Limitation for live presentations: literal application eliminates narrative tension — answer on slide one means no journey, no reason to stay engaged
- [[brian-rieb]]'s synthesis: use the pyramid to think, use the story to present

## Positioning (in the PMM space)

The document-communication standard. Applied to live presentations it conflicts with [[nancy-duarte]]'s narrative principles — the pyramid ensures logical rigor, the story ensures the audience cares. The two work together when the pyramid organizes the logic underneath a narrative arc.
