# Edward Tufte

## Overview

Statistician and data visualization theorist. Author of *The Cognitive Style of PowerPoint* (2006) and several foundational books on information design. Best known in presentation circles for arguing that slideware's default structures — particularly bullet-list hierarchies — actively degrade the quality of serious analysis.

## Key Facts

- *The Cognitive Style of PowerPoint: Pitching Out Corrupts Within* (2nd ed., Graphics Press, 2006)
- Most devastating example: Columbia shuttle disaster, where engineers communicated critical safety data through nested PowerPoint bullets that obscured severity — the format flattened information with life-or-death stakes
- Core argument: the problem isn't PowerPoint per se, it's the belief that slides carry the message
- Other major works: *The Visual Display of Quantitative Information*, *Envisioning Information*, *Visual Explanations*
- Known for self-publishing his books to control print quality

## Positioning (in the PMM space)

The intellectual case against bullet-heavy, text-dense slides. Complements [[garr-reynolds]] (design principles) and [[nancy-duarte]] (narrative) with a data-and-analysis lens: complex information requires better formats than slideware defaults provide.
