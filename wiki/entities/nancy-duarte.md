# Nancy Duarte

## Overview

Presentation designer and author. Best known for *Resonate* (2010), which reframes presentations as narrative structures with the audience as hero and the presenter as mentor. Identified the "what is / what could be" oscillation pattern in the most effective presentations and speeches.

## Key Facts

- *Resonate: Present Visual Stories that Transform Audiences* (Wiley, 2010)
- Core insight: the best presentations oscillate between current reality ("what is") and a better future ("what could be"), building tension and resolution until the audience is ready to act
- Audience = hero. Presenter = mentor. The presenter's job is to give the audience tools and motivation to change something — not to demonstrate how smart they are
- Analyzed speeches from Lincoln, Churchill, Jobs, King to identify the common narrative structure
- Also authored *slide:ology* (visual design of slides) and runs Duarte Inc. (presentation consulting)

## Positioning (in the PMM space)

The narrative and structure counterpart to [[garr-reynolds]]' design principles. Where Reynolds tells you what to put on the slide, Duarte tells you how to build the arc across the talk. Directly relevant to sales narratives, executive presentations, and keynotes.
