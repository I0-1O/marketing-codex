# April Dunford

## Overview

Product positioning consultant and author. Best known for *Obviously Awesome* (2019, expanded 2026), which defines a practical five-component positioning framework for B2B technology products. Widely referenced in PMM circles as the clearest practitioner framework for positioning work.

## Key Facts

- Author of *Obviously Awesome: How to Nail Product Positioning so Customers Get It, Buy It, Love It*
- Five-component positioning framework: competitive alternatives → differentiated capabilities → differentiated value → target customer → best-fit market frame
- Key insight relevant to [[benefit-ladder]]: most teams get stuck at "differentiated capabilities" and fail to translate them into what those capabilities mean for the buyer's world — the "so what for customers?" step
- Runs positioning workshops and consulting for B2B tech companies

## Positioning (in the PMM space)

The practical standard for positioning methodology in B2B SaaS and tech. Her framework is process-oriented and executable — not theoretical. Contrasts with more academic positioning approaches.

## Competitive Context

Referenced alongside [[clayton-christensen]] (Jobs to Be Done) and [[matthew-dixon-brent-adamson]] (Challenger Sale) as foundational frameworks for outcome-oriented marketing.
