# Garr Reynolds

## Overview

Author of *Presentation Zen* (2008, 3rd ed. 2019). Presentation designer and educator known for applying Japanese design principles — simplicity, restraint, negative space — to slide design and delivery. Coined the term "slideument" for the slide-document hybrid that fails at both jobs.

## Key Facts

- *Presentation Zen* (New Riders, 3rd ed. 2019): the most widely read book on presentation design
- Coined "slideument": a deck that tries to be both visual aid and standalone document, and succeeds at neither
- Core argument: the best slides *need* a presenter to work — that is a feature, not a flaw
- "Analog planning": start with paper and pen before touching software; separates thinking from formatting
- Drew on Japanese design (wabi-sabi, ma, simplicity) to argue that restraint in slides is a positive choice, not laziness

## Positioning (in the PMM space)

The practitioner standard for presentation design. Complements [[nancy-duarte]]'s narrative framework (story structure) with visual design principles (what goes on the slide and how it looks).
