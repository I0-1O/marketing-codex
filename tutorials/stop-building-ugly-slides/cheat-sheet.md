# Stop Building Ugly Slides — Cheat Sheet

*Not an exhaustive list. There are always exceptions to every rule.*

## Tip 1: 3 to 5 Bullets. That's It.
- 3 is ideal. 5 is the ceiling.
- If you hit 6, stop — you have two slides, not one.
- Cutting to 5 forces you to decide what actually matters.

## Tip 2: Less Is More
- Single words. Short phrases. Small sentences.
- Read the slide out loud. Does it sound like what you were going to say? Strip it back.
- Your job is to say the thing. The slide's job is to remind the room what you're talking about.
- No acronyms — wherever you have the chance, spell it out.

## Tip 3: One Slide. One Concept.
- One idea per slide. If you're saying "and also" — that's your next slide.
- Struggling? Ask yourself: "What am I trying to do with this slide?" If the answer is a list of things, it's too much.
- More slides is fine. Slides are free.
- If you have a master document, spreadsheet, or graphic — link to it.

## Tip 4: Use Slide Notes
Build speaker notes with these three sections. Better yet, have AI do it for you.
- **On This Slide** — what they're looking at and why you're showing it
- **What to Know** — supporting facts, context, likely questions
- **What to Say** — your talk track, written the way you'd say it out loud

---

## Bonus Tips
- A deck with solid speaker notes is often all you need as a leave-behind. The story is already in there.
- Use active voice on slides as much as possible. Avoid the passive.
- **AI workflow for building decks:**
  1. Build an outline (markdown works well)
  2. Fill in details and facts — now you have a master document, even if it's for 1–2 slides
  3. Use [[slide-deck|the AI skill]] to build the scaffolding and suggest related assets
  4. Review and refine — AI hallucinations and word salad are real
